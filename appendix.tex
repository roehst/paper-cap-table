
\subsection{Transactions}

\Glspl{transaction} are the actions performed on  \glspl{security}.  \Glspl{transaction} are organized in primitive \glspl{transaction} and specific transactions. Primitive \glspl{transaction} represent the different \textit{kinds} of \glspl{transaction} in the financial domain. Figure~\ref{fig:ocf-primitive-transactions} shows the directory structure of the primitive \glspl{transaction}.

\begin{figure}[H]
	\dirtree{%
		.1 schema/.
		.2 primitives/.
		.3 objects/.
		.4 transactions/.
		.5 acceptance/Acceptance.schema.json.
		.5 cancellation/Cancellation.schema.json.
		.5 conversion/Conversion.schema.json.
		.5 exercise/Exercise.schema.json.
		.5 issuance/Issuance.schema.json.
		.5 reissuance/Reissuance.schema.json.
		.5 release/Release.schema.json.
		.5 repurchase/Repurchase.schema.json.
		.5 retraction/Retraction.schema.json.
		.5 SecurityTransaction.schema.json.
		.5 StockClassTransaction.schema.json.
		.5 StockPlanTransaction.schema.json.
		.5 Transaction.schema.json.
		.5 transfer/Transfer.schema.json.
	}
	\caption{Primitive transactions in the OCF}\label{fig:ocf-primitive-transactions}
\end{figure}

The primitive \glspl{transaction}, with additional fields, are specialized to each of four different types of securities: stocks, convertible notes, warrants, and plan \glspl{security}. We group them by the \gls{asset-class} they specialize in. Figure~\ref{fig:ocf-transactions-specialized-vesting} shows the directory structure of the specialized \glspl{transaction}.
